Projects

BrainPainter

Brain visualization never looked so good! BrainPainter is a software for visualizing brain structures. We use the Blender computer graphics engine API to load and color brain structures. These renders of colored brain structures help neuroscientists and medical researchers show pathology progression overtime. This project is a part of the work I do at my internship at the MIT Computer Science & Artificial Intelligence Laboratory.

COVerage

COVerage is a project that displays COVID-19 news on a local level. COVerage is meant to help users keep up to date on how the COVID-19 pandemic has been adfecting their community. Through this project we used natural language processing techniques to optimize news for users based on their location and we were able to show COVID news from around the world. This project was a part of the work that I did as an intern at the Stanford Compression Forum and Electrical Engineering Department. Through this project my team and I were able to learn from industry and academic leading researchers working with computational journalism and artificial intelligence (specifically natural language processing). By the end of our internship my team and I produced a research publication detailing our application's creation and impact.

FRC Vision

As a programmer on my high school's robotics team (FRC 1683), I led the development of a computer vision system for our robot. The system included a ring light that shines light on retroreflective tape to identify targets. Then, the code our team wrote calculates the distance and angle to a target. By adding PID's to our robot's controls using the data it got from the camera, our robot was able to accurately follow targets.
